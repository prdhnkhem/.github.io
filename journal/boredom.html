We Have Forgotten How to Be Bored

Reflection | Sep 05, 2025

In the endless scroll, we have lost the sacred space of stillness. Boredom is not emptiness; it is the fertile ground where creativity and self-reflection are born. It is the necessary silence that allows our own inner voice to finally be heard above the noise of the world.

I see our addiction to distraction as one of the greatest diseases of our time. We reach for our phones in every spare momentâ€”waiting for a friend, sitting on a bus, even in the moments before sleep. We are terrified of being alone with our own thoughts.

"The cure is not another app or another distraction. The cure is simply to be present. To allow for the discomfort of boredom, and to see what wisdom emerges from it."

Try it. The next time you have a spare five minutes, do nothing. Don't check your notifications. Don't listen to music. Just sit. Or stand. Observe your breath. Look at the sky. Listen to the sounds around you. It will feel strange at first. It might even feel painful. But on the other side of that discomfort is a freedom that no amount of digital entertainment can ever provide.
