We Have Forgotten How to Be Bored

Reflection | Sep 05, 2025

In the endless scroll of modern life, we have lost one of the most sacred human spaces: the space of stillness. We have forgotten how to be bored. We treat boredom not as a state of rest, but as a problem to be solved with another notification, another video, another distraction.

But boredom is not emptiness. It is the fertile ground where creativity, self-reflection, and our own inner wisdom are born. It is the necessary silence that allows our soul's voice to finally be heard above the constant noise of the world. I see our collective addiction to distraction as one of the greatest, most subtle diseases of our time. We reach for our phones in every spare moment—waiting for a friend, sitting on a bus, even in the moments before sleep—because we are terrified of being alone with our own thoughts.

"The cure is not another app or another distraction. The cure is simply to be present. To allow for the discomfort of boredom, and to see what truth emerges from it."

I challenge you to try it. The next time you have a spare five minutes, do nothing. Don't check your notifications. Don't listen to music. Just sit. Or stand. Observe your breath. Look at the sky. Listen to the sounds of your environment. It will feel strange at first. Your mind will race, looking for its usual fix. It might even feel painful. But if you can stay with that discomfort, on the other side of it is a freedom and a clarity that no amount of digital entertainment can ever provide. In that space, you begin to remember who you are.
