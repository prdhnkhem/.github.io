The Core Team

Become a foundational member of the movement.

Hunate Engineers

Hunate Engineers are the core life designers of the movement. We are seeking a multi-disciplinary team of young, skilled, and dedicated individuals to co-create and implement Hunateculture designs. This is a deep commitment to walking the path and building the future.

Process & Criteria

Criteria: Applicants should be under 25, self-motivated, and possess skills in one of our 13 core domains (e.g., Permaculture, Vastu, Web & IT). Most importantly, they must be willing to learn, unlearn, and dedicate themselves to the movement's principles.

Process: The selection journey involves an initial application, a comprehensive "First Assignment" to test alignment and dedication, and a final deep-dive interview. Selected engineers will then participate in a 1-month incubator in Kathmandu to bond as a team and finalize plans before fieldwork begins.

Apply as an Engineer

Hunate Mentors

Mentors are the guardians of wisdom in our movement. We are seeking respected, experienced professionals and elders from each engineering domain to serve as our advisory council. Your role is to guide our young team, provide ethical oversight, and lend your credibility to the mission.

Process & Criteria

Criteria: Mentors are established professionals in their field, known for their integrity and wisdom. They resonate with our mission and are willing to offer guidance out of a spirit of service, not for financial gain.

Process: We engage potential mentors through a process of respectful dialogue. This involves an initial call to gauge philosophical alignment, followed by an in-person meeting where we formally present the vision and invite them to join. The relationship is based on mutual respect and a shared commitment to the future of Nepal.

Apply as a Mentor

Our Partners

Become a living example of a regenerative future.

Hunate Hub

We are searching for 7 families and 7 orphanages across Nepal to become our first Hunateculture Hubs. If you have land, a deep desire for self-reliance, and the courage to become a living example for your community, we invite you to apply.

Process & Criteria

Criteria: Partners must have secure access to at least 1 Bigha of land, a genuine resonance with the Hunate vision, and a clear understanding that this is a partnership of active participation, not a financial grant. A commitment to the multi-year journey is essential.

Process: After an initial application, shortlisted candidates will be visited by our team for an in-depth dialogue. This site visit allows us to understand the story of the land and the family. The final selection is a democratic process made by our Weighted Council, ensuring a fair and holistic decision.

Apply as a Hub

Hunate Volunteers

Process & Criteria

Criteria: We look for volunteers with practical skills, a resilient and adaptable spirit, and a deep alignment with our philosophy of service. A minimum 3-month commitment is required to ensure a meaningful contribution.

Process: The journey involves an application form, an alignment interview, and a comprehensive onboarding kit before arrival. Upon arrival, volunteers are integrated into the daily rhythm and project work of the hub, becoming true members of the community.

Apply as a Volunteer

District Representatives

District Representatives (DRs) are the living bridge between the national movement and local communities. We need one male and one female representative from each district to be our eyes, ears, and heart on the ground, helping us build trust, conduct local research, and identify potential partners.

Process & Criteria

Criteria: DRs must have deep local roots, social intelligence, and a strong philosophical alignment with Hunate. This is a role of service, based on trust and integrity.

Process: Candidates complete an application and a "First Assignment" designed to showcase their local insight. This is followed by an alignment interview. Selected DRs are then equipped with a training handbook and begin their work remotely, acting as our trusted community liaisons.
