Parents are Not Qualified

Why we bring life into the world without preparation or self-knowledge.

To operate a vehicle, you need a license. To practice medicine, you need a degree. To build a house, you need an engineer. Yet, to perform the most complex task in human history—raising another human being—there is zero systemic requirement for qualification. We have normalized the production of life as a casual byproduct of biological desire.

/ THE SURVIVAL TRAP

In the profit-based economy, parents are forced to spend their most vital hours chasing currency to pay for food, shelter, and debt. This survival pressure leaves zero room for the study of child psychology or the nature of human development. We have parents who are physically present but mentally and emotionally bankrupt, unable to offer the presence required for a child to flourish.

/ THE ABSENCE OF SELF-KNOWLEDGE

You cannot guide a soul if you do not understand your own. Most parents enter child-rearing while still trapped in their own mental loops, addictions, and trauma. Without first performing the "Research of the Self," parents simply pass their systemic friction down to the next generation. They do not raise children; they manufacture mirrors of their own unhealed shadows.

/ OUTSOURCING THE SOUL

Because they are unqualified and busy, parents outsource the education and values of their children to failing social structures: the factory-style school system and manipulative digital media. A parent who does not know how to teach their child the laws of nature has effectively surrendered their primary role as a guide.

SYSTEMIC CONCLUSION

Parenting must be recognized as a high-stakes discipline. Before giving birth, individuals must first detoxify themselves, understand their biological and cosmic timing, and master the basic skills of life (food, shelter, and emotion). We need a culture where giving birth is a conscious choice made by capable, multi-skilled researchers of life.
