The Story of a Vow

Every movement is born from a story. This is ours—a journey through chaos and silence, from a broken system to a new design for life.

Phase 1: Journey of Self-Discovery

(1991 - 2019) The Forging of a Visionary

The story of HUNATE begins not with a plan, but with a question. Born in the remote hills of Bhojpur to revolutionary parents who defied the caste system, my life was shaped by a legacy of rebellion. But their fight for a better world externally was mirrored by a fractured world at home. Sent to a hostel at a young age, I was disconnected from family, community, and myself.

This disconnection festered. By my late teens, I was lost—navigating addiction, stealing to fit in, and chasing a future I didn't believe in. The modern education system felt like a cage, teaching obedience but not purpose. My journey led me to the army, to multiple colleges, and deeper into a cycle of confusion and self-destruction. The turning point came with the ultimate loss: a friend died of a drug overdose while I was with him. The incident, and the jail time that followed, shattered my world and broke me open.

"My parents sent a message that I was better there in jail, and it broke me... I felt what freedom means. I wanted change."

This rock bottom became my foundation. I decided to change. I walked away from the life I knew and into a 10-day Vipassana meditation course. In the profound silence, I confronted my pain, my anger, and my grief. I didn't just find peace; I found myself. I cleansed myself from the inside out and emerged with a new vow: to understand the root of our collective suffering and to find a real, tangible way to heal.

My education began on the road. I worked as a laborer on trucks, traveling across Nepal and India. I listened to the stories of drivers, farmers, and prostitutes. I saw the corruption, the pollution, and the quiet dignity of people surviving within a broken system. I hitchhiked, volunteered on organic farms in Cambodia, and became the principal of a remote school, all in search of an answer. I was no longer running from something; I was running towards a new way of living.

Phase 2: The Joon Project

(2019 - 2023) Building a Dream from the Ground Up

The Joon Project was the first attempt to give this vision a home. As the COVID-19 pandemic froze the world, it became a call to action. With a group of friends in my hometown of Damak, we started with nothing but a piece of family land and donated tools. We built our first hut from mud, bamboo, and community spirit. This "place that seemed like nowhere" became our laboratory.

We lived by a simple rule: create with what you have. We built our own water systems, shelters, and even an organic, door-less toilet. When a storm destroyed our tent, we rebuilt it the next day, stronger and with more laughter. The project was never just about structures; it was about connection. We hosted free live music sessions that united local youth, and we partnered with a local orphanage, not just to help, but to become family—introducing yoga, meditation, and a system of self-responsibility that we called "The Joon Way."

"The Joon Camp Festival was a revolution of hearts. We didn’t sell tickets. We didn’t ask for sponsors. Still... everything happened. When people come together with the right intention, magic unfolds."

For several years, The Joon Project thrived as an international hub, welcoming volunteers from around the world. We shared skills, stories, and cultures, experimenting with a life less dependent on money and more on mutual trust. But in 2023, the project came to an abrupt halt when a co-founder's father suffered a severe accident. The community's energy shifted entirely to his care. It was a painful, necessary pause that forced a deep reflection.

Phase 3: The Birth of HUNATE

(2023 - Present) From an Experiment to a Blueprint

The halt of The Joon Project was not an end, but a transformation. It was a time to distill the raw, passionate experimentation of Joon into a mature, strategic, and scalable vision. I realized that while Joon (meaning "Moon") was about starting fresh, the next phase needed to be about returning to the community with a clear blueprint for healing.

This is HUNATE. It carries all the lessons from my personal journey and the hands-on experience of The Joon Project. It is the synthesis of ancient wisdom and modern ethics, designed not just to create one isolated community, but to provide a replicable system—Hunateculture—that any family, orphanage, or community can use to reclaim their health, purpose, and sovereignty.

My story is not unique. It is the story of a generation feeling lost in a system that no longer serves us. HUNATE is the response. It is a vow to stop just talking about the problems and to start building the solution, one family, one village, one act of courage at a time.

The story continues. Now, understand the vision it created.
