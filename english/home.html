A WORLD IN CRISIS. A BLUEPRINT FOR RENEWAL.

We are living in a failing system. The Hunate Project is a dedicated research initiative to design and implement a regenerative, nature-based alternative for our families, communities, and the world.

DISCOVER THE BLUEPRINT

THE FAILING SYSTEM: A QUESTION OF SURVIVAL

Why do we send our children to schools for 15 years, only for them to graduate into a world of debt and uncertainty? Why do we work our entire lives building homes we barely live in, for families we hardly see? We are taught to chase success, but what is success in a world where the air is polluted, the food is toxic, and the community is fractured?

These are not failures of individuals, but failures of a system designed for extraction, not regeneration. We have built a world that profits from our disconnectionâ€”from nature, from our communities, and from our own inner wisdom. The evidence is all around us: rising mental illness, ecological collapse, and a deep, pervasive sense of meaninglessness.

"We have become experts in the art of making a living, but amateurs in the art of living."

The Hunate Project begins by asking the forbidden questions and daring to challenge the foundations of a society that is no longer serving its people or the planet. It is a declaration that a different way is not only possible, but essential for our collective survival and thrival.

OUR STORY: FROM A PERSONAL JOURNEY TO A GLOBAL MOVEMENT

The Hunate Project was not born in a boardroom; it was forged in the fires of personal discovery and social experimentation. It began with the journey of one individual, Khem Raj Pradhan, seeking to understand the roots of suffering and the nature of a meaningful life (Phase 1).

This personal quest evolved into "The Joon Project," a bold social experiment where a diverse group of friends came together to brainstorm and build a new way of living, exploring the harmony between humanity, nature, and technology (Phase 2). After years of hands-on work and invaluable lessons, the project entered a critical phase of reflection and deep planningâ€”"The Pause" (Phase 3). It is from this fertile ground of experience and insight that the comprehensive Hunate blueprint has emerged, ready to be shared with the world.
