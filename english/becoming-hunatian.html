Becoming Hunatian

Next Generation Human The World Needs

Hunateculture provides the map for a new world, but the "Becoming Hunatian" path builds the traveler who is strong, wise, and resilient enough for the journey. This is the essential inner work, the internal blueprint for a new kind of human.

Why To Be Hunatian?

Because this world needs you to be the best version of you as Humans living on Earth. Simple. Look at the failing social system we have created and are living in. It's killing us humans, it's killing nature, it's killing the future. It's crazy and this world needs a human who sees these things, feels these things happening, and who can change this system. In this era, look how this failing system has made everything chaotic, with distractions everywhere, manipulation, influences, war, poverty, and greed. We are trapped inside a system that doesn't allow us time to think, reflect, or question our own existence, let alone find our purpose.

This world needs such humans who know it all, who see the loop, who see the hole, who see the problems in this world, who are willing to find answers to these problems, who can sacrifice self-desires for a better future.

Parents start raising us, pushing us towards their wishes and demands. Friends call us to party or do this and that. We are programmed from birth. Becoming Hunatian is the conscious act of unplugging from that programming, of creating the space to find your own truth, and of building the strength to live by it.

Way To Be Human: The Complete Curriculum

  1. 1st Task: Find Your Space

    For the next 21 days, find a private, clean, and silent space. This is your sanctuary. Ensure no one can disturb you here. This space is where you will practice becoming the witness to your own mind and body.

  2. 2nd Task: Sit Still

    Every morning before sunrise and every evening before sleep, for a minimum of 30 minutes, sit still in your space. Sit in a comfortable posture, but keep your spine straight. The simple act of sitting still is a rebellion against the modern world's demand for constant motion.

  3. 3rd Task: Focus on Breath

    While sitting, bring your entire focus to your breath. Observe the natural, normal breath as it enters and leaves your nostrils. Don't try to change it. Just observe. Your mind will wander a thousand times. Your task is to gently, without frustration, bring it back to the breath a thousand and one times. This is the fundamental training for a focused mind.

  4. 4th Task: Think About the Body

    After your breath meditation, spend time thinking about your own body. Contemplate its complexity, its fragility, its needs. Think about what you eat, what you drink, and how you treat this precious vehicle. Acknowledge it as the only tool you truly have to experience this life.

  5. 5th Task: Simple Exercises

    Engage in at least 30 minutes of simple, conscious exercise daily. This could be yoga, stretching, walking, or any activity that connects your mind to the physical sensations of your body. Move with awareness.

  6. 6th Task: Sense Organs: Tongue & Food

    For 21 days, become a scientist of your own consumption. Before you eat, ask: Is this food good for my body? Where did it come from? How was it made? Eat slowly, consciously, and in silence if possible. Notice how different foods affect your energy, your mood, and your thoughts. This is the first step in reclaiming your health.

  7. 7th Task: Sense Organs: Skin & Care

    Pay attention to what you put on your skin—soaps, lotions, clothes. Are they natural or synthetic? Do they nourish or irritate your body's largest organ? Practice conscious care for your skin.

  8. 8th Task: Protect Ears

    Be mindful of the sounds you allow into your awareness. Avoid gossip, negative news, and meaningless noise. Seek out silence, or the sounds of nature. Your ears are a gateway to your mind; guard them well.

  9. 9th Task: Sense Organs: Eye and Vision

    Become conscious of what you look at. The images you see—on screens, in advertisements—program your mind. Practice looking at nature, at art, at the faces of loved ones. See the world directly, not just through a screen.

  10. 10th Task: Sense Organs: Nose & Smell

    Use your sense of smell. Pay attention to the aroma of your food, the scent of the earth after rain, the fragrance of flowers. Our sense of smell is a powerful, primal connection to the world that is often ignored.

  11. 11th Task: Sense Organs: Fasting & Intuition

    After 21 days of conscious practice, undertake a fast—from information, from speaking, and perhaps from food for a short period (with proper guidance). This deep cleansing of the senses sharpens your intuition, your "sixth sense," which is the voice of your inner wisdom.

  12. 12th Task: Recall Before Sleep

    Every night before you sleep, review your day from beginning to end, like watching a movie. Observe your actions, your words, and your thoughts without judgment. Acknowledge your mistakes and your successes. This practice builds self-awareness and allows you to consciously design a better tomorrow.

  13. 13th Task: Harvest Multiple Skills, As Humans

    Break free from the trap of specialization. Learn a variety of practical, real-world skills. Learn to grow food, to cook, to build, to fix things, to play music, to speak a new language. A resilient human is a multi-skilled human.

A Note on Your Journey

This path is not easy. It is a series of challenges designed to break down the old and build up the new. You may feel confused, lost, or stuck at times. This is a natural part of the process. If you find yourself in need of guidance, you are not alone.

The Goal: Embodying the Next Generation Human

The person who completes this journey is not the same person who began it. They are a "Next Generation Human"—self-aware, multi-skilled, and emotionally resilient. They are grounded in their purpose and connected to the natural world. They have detoxed from the addiction to distraction and have cultivated a mind that is clear, focused, and creative. They are not merely survivors of the failing system; they are the conscious architects of its replacement. They are, in short, ready.
