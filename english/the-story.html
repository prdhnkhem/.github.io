Our Story

The Hunate Project is not a theoretical exercise; it is a living blueprint forged through decades of real-world experience. Our story unfolds in two distinct, foundational chapters: a profound personal quest for truth, and a bold collective experiment to build a new way of living.

PHASE 1: A PERSONAL JOURNEY OF SELF-DISCOVERY

The origins of Hunate lie in a deep, personal quest to understand the roots of meaning in a world of growing disconnection. It is the story of Khem Raj Pradhan, a journey that began not with a business plan, but with fundamental questions about life's purpose. This path led him away from conventional success and into a world of intense learning—through Vipassana meditation, travels across India, and hands-on work in organic farms and ashrams. It was a period of shedding societal conditioning to find the universal principles of balance that govern nature and our inner lives. This phase was about deconstruction—breaking down old paradigms to discover the timeless foundation upon which a regenerative culture could be built.

PHASE 2: THE JOON PROJECT — A SOCIAL EXPERIMENT

"From these humble beginnings, we began to explore the harmony between humanity, nature, and technology."

A personal journey, no matter how profound, must be tested in the crucible of community. Phase two saw the birth of "The Joon Project," where the philosophy moved from the individual to the collective. In 2015, a diverse group of passionate friends—geologists, engineers, artists, and thinkers—came together, united by a shared discontent with the status quo. What began as conversations on whiteboards evolved into a real-world social experiment. The group started building a physical platform in Damak in 2019, turning abstract ideas about community, technology, and nature into tangible structures and systems. It was a critical, hands-on phase of learning, collaboration, and discovering the practical challenges and immense possibilities of building a new world from the ground up.
