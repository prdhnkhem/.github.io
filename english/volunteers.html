The Volunteers: The Hands of the Movement

Volunteering with the Hunate Movement is not tourism; it is a deep and committed act of co-creation. This is an invitation for skilled individuals to join us on the ground to build, teach, learn, and grow as part of a functioning regenerative community.

A Call for Builders, Not Dreamers

We are immensely grateful for every person who resonates with our vision, but to build a new world, we need more than just good intentions. We need skills. This is a call for experienced, hands-on individuals who are ready to contribute their expertise in a meaningful way. We are not looking for "lazy dreamers or people looking to crash while traveling." We are looking for dedicated co-creators.

To honor this commitment, we require a minimum 3-month stay for all volunteers. This ensures you have enough time to truly integrate into the community and make a significant contribution. Life at a Hunate Hub is a self-sustaining model. Your contribution, which covers your food, shelter, and basic facilities, allows us to continue our work without depending on outside funding. This is a journey of shared responsibility and mutual support.

The Expertise We Need on the Ground

Wood Experts (Carpenters)

Skilled in house building, furniture making, and other forms of fine woodworking.

Plumbers & Electricians

Experienced in setting up irrigation, drainage, and off-grid electrical systems.

Renewable Energy Experts

Knowledgeable in installing and maintaining solar, biogas, and greywater filtration systems.

Natural Builders

Masters of building with earth-friendly materials like mud, clay, cob, and stone.

Farmers & Gardeners

Experienced in permaculture, organic farming, and creating productive food ecosystems.

Constructors & Builders

Generalists with a strong background in construction and managing building projects.

Project Site Managers

Organized leaders who can manage daily tasks, coordinate teams, and keep projects on track.

Videographers & Storytellers

Creative individuals who can document our journey, edit videos, and handle social media to share our story with the world.

Our Sacred Agreements: The Vows of a Hunate Volunteer

To create a safe, respectful, and transformative environment for everyone, we ask every volunteer to agree to these five vows during their stay:

  • The Vow of Presence: Be here, now. Minimize digital distractions during work and communal times. Engage with the people and the place in front of you.
  • The Vow of Open Communication: If a conflict arises, address it directly and respectfully. We do not engage in gossip.
  • The Vow of Respect for Local Culture: You are a guest. We ask you to dress modestly, be mindful of local customs, and interact with the community with humility.
  • The Vow of Clean Living: To maintain a clear environment, the use of illegal drugs and excessive alcohol is not permitted at our hubs.
  • The Vow of Ahiṃsā (Non-Harm): We commit to causing no harm—physically, verbally, or emotionally—to people, animals, or the land.
